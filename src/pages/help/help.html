How to access the home page of the website panel?

Clicking the logo of MY Assignment Services would take you to the home page of your in-website panel. You can use this to access and track each and everything each about your assignment. The mode of payment, the channel of payment, amount due, amount to be paid, whether you have opted for partial payment or not; can all be clearly visible to the client here.

My Assignment Services has made additional efforts in order to make the panel as user friendly as possible. Its simplistic nature is to save time and offer all its clients as much convenience as possible.

Elements on Home Page

My Assignments

Clicking on My Assignments would take the client to the window below(refer image).

In the image, it can clearly be seen that there are 7 columns that need the client’s attention.

Order Name

This is the Order ID (or Name) that was assigned to your order. It might be a nae or a code starting with “MAS”.

Status

Paid : The payment for the client’s order has been made.

New Inquiry : The client’s interest has been recorded as a new inquiry.

Unassigned : The search for the best suitable experts is still underway in order to guarantee the client the best grades.

Done : The assignment solution is ready and shall soon be quality checked and delivered.

Delivered (P) : The solution has been delivered to the concerned client’s email ID. However, there is still an amount to be paid since the “(P)” stands for “Partial Payment”.

Delivered : The assignment solution has been delivered and the client may check his/her email ID if not already done.

Word Count

The word count that was specified at the time of placing the order.

Total/Paid Amount

This is exactly as represented. It is written as the Total Amount followed by the amount to be paid.

IN the above example,

the total amount = AUD100

Paid Amount = 10

In this case, the balance to be paid by the client is AUD90.

Subject

This is the subject domain that your assignment belongs to.

Deadline

This is the maximum deadline that the client demands to receive the assignment solution by.
